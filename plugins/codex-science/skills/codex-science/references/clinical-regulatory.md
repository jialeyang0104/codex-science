# Clinical And Regulatory Workflow

Use this route for clinical study design, trial protocols, endpoint definitions, inclusion/exclusion criteria, sample size planning, clinical evidence summaries, and regulatory-facing documentation.

## Process

1. Identify product/intervention, indication, population, comparator, endpoints, and jurisdiction.
2. Look for analogous trials and guidance when current sources are required.
3. Draft protocol sections in a structured order: rationale, objectives, design, population, interventions, endpoints, statistics, operations, safety, ethics.
4. Keep assumptions explicit.
5. Treat medical advice, FDA/NIH compliance, and regulatory claims as high-stakes: verify before finalizing.

## Specialist Skill

Prefer `clinical-trial-protocol-skill` for protocol generation.

## Indication Dossier Route

When the user asks for a disease or therapeutic indication landscape, build an indication dossier before drafting trial strategy:

1. Resolve the indication identity as a patient population, not just a disease label.
2. Separate diagnosis, biology, trial-enrollable population, and regulatory indication.
3. Research epidemiology, biology, standard of care, unmet need, regulatory precedent, endpoints, and landmark trials.
4. Use current sources: PubMed, ClinicalTrials.gov, FDA/EMA, disease society guidelines, and major treatment guidelines.
5. Write structured waypoints for long dossiers so the work can resume cleanly.
