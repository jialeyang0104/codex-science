# Literature And Evidence Workflow

Use this route for paper reading, literature review, evidence synthesis, gap analysis, citation support, and manuscript argument building.

## Process

1. Determine whether the task needs current literature. If yes, use web or configured MCP/search tools.
2. Prefer primary sources, guidelines, trial registries, and full-text PDFs over secondary summaries.
3. Extract study type, population, exposure/intervention, endpoint, methods, limitations, and reusable claims.
4. Distinguish evidence from inference.
5. Produce one of: evidence table, annotated outline, claim-to-source map, narrative synthesis, or manuscript-ready paragraphs.

## Quality Gates

- Do not invent citations.
- Mark inaccessible full text.
- Keep clinical recommendations tied to guidelines or trials.
- State date of search for current topics.
