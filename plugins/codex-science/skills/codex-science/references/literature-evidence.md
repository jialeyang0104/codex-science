# Literature And Evidence Workflow

Use this route for paper reading, literature review, evidence synthesis, gap analysis, citation support, and manuscript argument building.

## Process

1. Determine whether the task needs current literature. If yes, use web or configured MCP/search tools before drafting.
2. Prefer primary sources, guidelines, trial registries, and full-text PDFs over secondary summaries.
3. Resolve DOI or registry identifiers instead of guessing citation details.
4. For broad reviews, expand around the top papers by checking backward references and forward cited-by papers.
5. Extract study type, population, exposure/intervention, endpoint, methods, limitations, and reusable claims.
6. Distinguish evidence from inference.
7. Produce one of: evidence table, annotated outline, claim-to-source map, narrative synthesis, or manuscript-ready paragraphs.

## Quality Gates

- Do not invent citations.
- Do not use citation lists as a substitute for synthesis; organize around scientific claims and disagreements.
- Mark inaccessible full text.
- Check retraction or major correction status for surprising, clinical, or high-profile claims.
- Keep clinical recommendations tied to guidelines or trials.
- State date of search for current topics.
- Use markdown DOI links when sources have DOIs.

For multi-page PDFs, figure extraction, manuscript narrative, and publication figures, also read `literature-document-figure.md`.
